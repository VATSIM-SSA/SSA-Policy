# Regional Virtual Airline (rVA) Partner Policy

**Effective Date**: 01 February 2025  
**Version**: 0.1

## 1. Policy Introduction

This policy outlines how VATSSA can support and collaborate with Regional Virtual Airlines (rVAs) operating within its jurisdiction. It does **not** replace or supersede VATSIM’s Virtual Airline Associate Policy or VATSIM’s VA Partner Policy.

The purpose of the policy is to:

- Enhance visibility and establish event-related partnerships for rVAs.
- Maintain an up-to-date inventory of rVAs, including their owners and key details.
- Guide rVAs towards becoming VATSIM-partnered entities.

To apply for VATSSA rVA partnership, complete the application form on the VATSSA website. The **Virtual Airline Relationship Manager** will follow up after submission.

---

## 2. Roles and Responsibilities

### 2.1 VATSSA

VATSSA will appoint a **Virtual Airline Relationship Manager**, responsible for:

- Following up on rVA registrations.
- Maintaining an active inventory of rVAs.
- Ensuring rVAs meet policy requirements.
- Coordinating with tech and social teams for displaying rVA information and logos.
- Coordinating with the events team regarding event benefits.

### 2.2 Regional Virtual Airline (rVA)

**Owners** of rVAs must:

- Register their rVA with VATSSA to apply for Partner status.
- Inform the VATSSA Relationship Manager of any changes in ownership or registration.
- Register and maintain rVA **Event Managers**, if applicable.

**Event Managers**, once registered, may coordinate rVA events directly with VATSSA’s Event Team.

---

## 3. Requirements

For rVA eligibility, the following are required:

- A **unique name** distinct from other VATSSA-affiliated rVAs.
- Provide valid **email addresses**, full **VATSIM registered names**, and **CIDs** for the primary contact, owner, and Event Managers.
- Notify VATSSA promptly of any email changes.
- Provide **ICAO callsign codes** and **radio callsign lists** used on the network, and update VATSSA if they change.
- Encourage pilots to fly on VATSIM professionally and abide by the VATSIM Code of Conduct.
- If the rVA has a website, supply the URL and keep VATSSA updated of additions or changes.

---

## 4. Partner Tiers

VATSSA offers three partnership tiers depending on rVA maturity and alignment with VATSSA airspace:

### 4.1 Tier III – Regional VA (Growing rVAs)

**Additional Requirements:**

1. Registered or created **at least 90 days** prior to application (e.g., via domain or forum creation date).
2. Completed **at least one online VATSIM flight** in the past 15 days (proof may be requested).
3. Main hubs located **within VATSSA airspace**, with most flights routing through VATSSA. Provide home country and hub information, and notify VATSSA of any changes.

**Benefits:**

- Visibility on the VATSSA website and Discord server.
- Participation in multi-rVA VATSSA-hosted events.

### 4.2 Tier II – Regional VA (Established rVAs)

**Additional Requirements:**

- Minimum of **5 active pilots** (each having completed at least one VATSIM flight in the past 30 days).
- Hubs and home country within VATSSA airspace (as above).

**Benefits:**

- Full visibility on the VATSSA website and Discord.
- Permission to display the VATSSA logo on rVA platforms.
- Participation in general VATSSA-hosted rVA events.
- Participation in an **annual VATSSA-hosted event dedicated exclusively** to the rVA.

### 4.3 Tier I – Regional VA (Active VATSIM Partner rVAs)

**Additional Requirements:**

- Must be an active **VATSIM Virtual Airline Partner (VAP)**.
- Hubs and home country within VATSSA airspace (as above).

**Benefits:**

- Highest visibility on VATSSA website and Discord.
- VATSSA logo display permission.
- Participation in general VATSSA-hosted rVA events.
- Participation in **biannual VATSSA-hosted events dedicated exclusively** to the rVA.

## 5. Policy Governance

### 5.1 Maintenance

This policy must be reviewed and updated:

- Following significant changes in VATSIM or VATSSA policies.
- At least **annually**.

### 5.2 Audit

Compliance documentation should be maintained, including:

- Spot checks and archival records of registrations.
- Quarterly reviews and any required remediation actions.

### 5.3 Supporting Material

May include:

- Detailed procedures, guidelines, checklists, flowcharts, or models to aid implementation.

### 5.4 Distribution

The policy must be:

- Available on the VATSSA website for all members.
- Communicated via Discord.
